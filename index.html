DR Khalil Ibrahim
    Birth
  • Ibrahim was born in Sudan in 1957.
  • Ibrahim was fromZaghawa ethnic group,which is located mainly in Sudan, with a minority on the Chad side of the border<
    Early life
  • He was an enthusiastic supporter of the National Islamic Front (NIF) seizure of power under the direction of Islamist Hassan al-Turabi in 1989.
  • He also served as the state minister for education in Darfur between 1991 and 1994 in al-Fashir, North Darfur.
  • Dr. Ibrahim went on to serve as the state minister for social affairs in Blue Nile in 1997 before a post as advisor to the governor of Southern Sudan in Juba in 1998.
  • He quit the post in August 1998, several months before the end of his appointment, and formed an NGO called "Fighting Poverty.
  • He did his Masters in Public Health at Universiteit Maastricht.
  • He published the Black Book in 2000, claiming that riverine Arabs dominated political power and resources.
    His Rebel life
  • In August 2001, Ibrahim published a press release from the Netherlands, in which he announced the formation of the Justice and Equality Movement.
  • brahim received political and financial support from Libya and its leader Muammar Gaddafi.
  • After the NTC's win in the 2011 Libyan civil war against the government of the Jamahiriya he was forced to flee back to Darfur
    His death
  • The Sudan Armed Forces announced that it had killed Ibrahim with an air strike in North Kordofan on 25 December 2011.
